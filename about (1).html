About us

Built by engineers
who got burned.

We've all been in that postmortem where someone says "we tried this two years ago and it killed the API." Grassion exists so that conversation never needs to happen again.

Every engineering team carries invisible institutional knowledge. It lives in Slack threads that get archived, in PR comments that get buried, in incident runbooks that no one reads until something breaks again. The moment that knowledge becomes inaccessible — when a senior engineer leaves, when the team doubles in size, when the codebase outgrows the people who originally built it — the same mistakes start repeating.

We started Grassion because we experienced this directly. An engineer on our team spent four days debugging an outage, only to discover the root cause was a config value that had been intentionally set the way it was after a nearly identical incident eighteen months earlier. The knowledge existed. It was just inaccessible at the moment it mattered most.

Grassion puts that knowledge back where it belongs — inside the pull request, at the exact moment a change is being made. Not in a wiki nobody reads. Not in a Slack channel that requires archaeology. Right there, before the merge button is clicked.

We're a small team. We move fast. We build things that work. We believe the best developer tools are the ones that feel like they were always supposed to exist — so obvious you wonder why nobody built it sooner.

What we believe

01
Tools should work where you already work

Grassion lives inside GitHub pull requests. No new dashboards to check. No new workflows to adopt. It fits into what you already do.

02
Context beats data

A warning with no context is noise. Grassion tells you not just what is risky — but why the original decision was made, with the full reasoning behind it.

03
Simple first, complex only when needed

The free tier does one thing extremely well. The pro tier unlocks depth when you need it. We don't over-engineer on day one.

04
Privacy is not an afterthought

Your code and your incidents are sensitive. We process only what's necessary. We never store raw code. We don't sell data. Ever.
